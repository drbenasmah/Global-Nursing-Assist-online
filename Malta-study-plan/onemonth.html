Week 1: Foundation Skills

Day 1: Professional Foundations

Day 6: GI & GU Basics

Week 2: Advanced Procedures

Complex nursing skills and emergency procedures

Day 8: Controlled Drugs (CDs)

Day 9: IV & Blood Transfusions

Day 10: Post-Op & Musculoskeletal

Day 11: Medical Emergencies (Pt 1)

Day 12: Medical Emergencies (Pt 2)

Day 13: Cardio & Neuro Emergencies

Day 14: Weekly Review

Focus on scenario questions. Talk through your response to a patient fall, an aggressive patient, and a patient with sudden shortness of breath.

Week 3: Practice & Application

Mock interviews and structured practice sessions

Days 15-17: SAFE/SBAR Practice

Each day, take 3-4 real recall questions. Verbally answer each one using a clear structure (Safety, Assessment, First Actions, Explain Rationale).

Day 18: Ethics & Communication

Day 19: Mock Interview #1

Day 20: Refine & Review

Based on your mock interview, identify your weak spots and review those topics. Do a light review of flashcards over the weekend.

Week 4: Final Preparation

Final polish and interview readiness

Day 21: Mock Interview #2

Ask a friend or colleague to quiz you. The pressure of a live audience is invaluable practice.

Day 22: Final Content Polish

Go back one last time to any topic you feel hesitant about. Focus on high-stakes procedures (CDs, Blood Transfusions).

Day 23: Prepare Your "Bookends"

Prepare a concise answer to "Tell me about yourself" and 2-3 thoughtful questions to ask the interviewers.

Day 24: Full Dress Rehearsal

Set up your interview space exactly as it will be. Wear your interview clothes. Do a final, short mock interview.

Days 25-27: Final Review & REST

Do a light review of notes on Day 25. Then REST. Your preparation is complete. Get good sleep, eat well, and relax. A rested mind performs best.
